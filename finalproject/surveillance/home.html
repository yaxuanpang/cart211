What is Surveillance Capitalism?

Surveillance capitalism is when companies make money by watching, collecting and analyzing what people do online. Data is collected through clicks, searches and habits, often without explicit user consent. Companies will use this information to predict user behavior and then sell these predictions to advertisers or other companies who will promote ads to influence user behavior. It turns private information into a product that companies can sell and profit from.

Companies use techniques such as cookies and IP (Internet Protocol) tracking to collect user information. The Internet of Things amplifies this issue, as everyday devices are now also capable of collecting and transmitting data about user’s preferences and online behavior.

What is the Internet of Things (IofT): Everyday objects such as a smartphone, Smart fridges, smartwatches, security cameras, smart thermostats, AirTags, and even cars that connect to apps can collect data and share it with other devices without user action.

It is based on the idea that online activity is never truly private and that consumer data can be collected without consent, knowledge or compensation for companies to make a profit by either selling this data or selling access to user data.
