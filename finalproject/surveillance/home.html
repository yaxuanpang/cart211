What is Surveillance Capitalism?

Surveillance capitalism is when companies make money by watching, collecting and analyzing what people do online. Data is collected through clicks, searches and habits, often without explicit user consent. Companies will use this information to predict user behavior and then sell these predictions to advertisers or other companies who will promote ads to influence user behavior. It turns private information into a product that companies can sell and profit from.

Companies use techniques such as cookies and IP (Internet Protocol) tracking to collect user information. The Internet of Things amplifies this issue, as everyday devices are now also capable of collecting and transmitting data about user’s preferences and online behavior.

What is the Internet of Things (IofT): Everyday objects such as a smartphone, Smart fridges, smartwatches, security cameras, smart thermostats, AirTags, and even cars that connect to apps can collect data and share it with other devices without user action.

It is based on the idea that online activity is never truly private and that consumer data can be collected without consent, knowledge or compensation for companies to make a profit by either selling this data or selling access to user data.

Importance of Data Privacy and Security

Data Privacy:

The proper handling of personal data to maintain an individual's privacy rights. It involves the collection, storage, use, and sharing of personal information in a way that is secure and ethical. It ensures that sensitive data is protected from unauthorized access or misuse.

Data Security:

The actions taken to protect data from unauthorized access, theft, or corruption. It involves implementing technical controls, such as encryption, access controls, and firewalls, to secure data from cyber threats

Protects Individuals

Data privacy protects our personal information from falling into the wrong hands. It gives individuals control over their data and ensures that this information remains confidential and secure, preventing misuse or exploitation. Data privacy plays a vital role in preventing identity theft and fraud. Cybercriminals often exploit vulnerabilities in data security to gain unauthorized access to personal information, which can lead to financial losses, reputational damage, and emotional distress. By prioritizing data privacy, it can reduce the risk of malicious activities and protect individuals from the consequences of identity theft.

Data privacy protects sensitive information including financial records, medical histories, and personal communication from cybercriminals or companies for exploitation. It gives individuals agency and control over what information is shared.

Protects Organizations

Data breaches can also have devastating effects on businesses, both financially and reputationally. Companies that suffer data breaches often face costly investigations, legal fees, and potential fines from regulatory bodies. Additionally, they may lose customer trust and loyalty. In some cases, data breaches have even forced companies to file for bankruptcy or cease operations altogether. These high-profile data breaches serve as reminders of the importance of data privacy and the need for robust security measures to protect sensitive information. They also highlight the significant consequences that can arise when personal data falls into the wrong hands.
