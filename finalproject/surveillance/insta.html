Instagram

It knows a lot more than what you post

Instagram collects information about what users look at, how long users look at a post, what users scroll past, what makes them stop, the accounts they visit, messaging patterns as well as the user's face, location, interests, how their mood changes depending on the content, even social network and relationships. It collects emotional and aesthetic preferences, which is the behavioral surplus. Instagram does not need to know this information to improve the platform, the platform needs it to make money by selling this information to those who want it.

The system does not only learn about what users like, it learns about the changes in user behavior. This can be turned into predictions that become the main product Instagram offers. The product is not just predictions, but it is also the ability to shape future behavior and future actions. actions.

Instagram is extremly good at behavioral targeting and gradually shapes an identity into clickable and, most importantly, sellable online persona that will react well to targeted ads and other targeted content.
